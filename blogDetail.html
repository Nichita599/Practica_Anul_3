1) Get two buckets for washing : Yes, that's right – two buckets. One bucket is filled with soapy water to rinse your sponge and the other to wash your car. Because if you rinse and wash in the same bucket, you will simply clean your car with dirty water.

2) Wash the tires first : The tires are one of the dirtiest parts of your car, so wash them first and then you can get cleaner water for the rest of the car.

3) Work from the top down : Working from the top down ensures that you can rinse the car without splashing soapy water on the areas you just cleaned, and since the bottom of the car is often the dirtiest, it prevents that dirt and grime from spreading to the rest of the car. Also, when washing windows, try to use vertical strokes on the inside of the windows and horizontal strokes on the outside of the windows. So if you get stripes, you know which side they are on!

4) Use the right soap : One of the tools that help to clean without damaging the paint is car soaps. The ingredients in the soap help to clean without damaging the paint. It is recommended not to use other soaps that are not intended for this purpose.
